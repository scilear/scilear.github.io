1. Macro Update & Sentiment

Global Market Tone: US and European markets experienced a notable downturn, reflecting heightened global anxieties. The primary driver for this negative sentiment appears to be the escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have significantly impacted global stability and economic outlook.

  • US Indices: Dow Jones, NASDAQ, S&P 500, and Russell 2000 all closed down on March 3rd, with losses ranging from -0.83% to -1.79%. This indicates a broad-based market retreat.
  • European Indices: FTSE 100, CAC 40, DAX 40, and FTSE MIB plunged further on March 4th, with losses between -2.75% and -3.92%. European markets are particularly sensitive to the Middle East conflict due to its direct impact on energy supplies and trade routes.
  • Commodities: Gold and silver prices surged, acting as traditional safe-haven assets amidst market uncertainty. Silver saw a significant increase of 3.42%, while gold rose by 1.61%. This upward trend in precious metals reflects investor flight to safety and concerns over inflation.
  • Geopolitical Risks: The escalating conflict in the Middle East, following a US-Israel strike on Iran and subsequent retaliation, is disrupting key trade routes and global energy flows. This has led to increased fuel costs, shipping delays, and renewed inflationary pressures, posing systemic risks to the global economy and potentially delaying central bank interest rate cuts.

3. Sector Deep Dives

Theme of the Week: Geopolitical Instability and Inflationary Pressures

The dominant theme this week is the significant impact of geopolitical instability in the Middle East on global markets and the subsequent inflationary pressures. The conflict's disruption of trade routes and energy supplies has led to a surge in commodity prices, particularly oil, and has renewed concerns about persistent inflation. This environment favors safe-haven assets and sectors resilient to supply chain shocks, while cyclical and growth-oriented sectors may face headwinds due to economic uncertainty and potential delays in interest rate cuts.
