3. Sector Deep Dives

Theme of the Week: AI Infrastructure & Quantum Computing

  • The AI buildout continues to be a dominant theme, with significant capital flowing into infrastructure, chips, and power generation.
  • **Quantum Computing:** Infleqtion (INFQ) is highlighted as a leader in scalable, room-temperature quantum computing, attracting partnerships with NVIDIA and government contracts.
  • **Space Infrastructure:** AST SpaceMobile (ASTS) is advancing direct-to-cell space broadband, with potential for AI capabilities in space and defense contracts. SpaceX's upcoming IPO is expected to validate the sector.
  • **AI Power & Data Centers:** Data center developers are outbidding home builders for land in some regions (e.g., Northern Virginia). Foreign capital (e.g., Japan) is funding US energy projects to support AI demand, making baseload power a strategic asset.
  • **AI Software & Monetization:** While AI enthusiasm is high, the market is scrutinizing how AI translates into revenue and cash. Companies like Figma are showing how to charge for AI usage, while others face pressure on workflow budgets as AI can replace traditional SaaS.
  • **Semiconductors:** Meta is expanding its partnership with Nvidia beyond GPUs to full-stack AI supply (CPUs, racks, networking), indicating a shift towards infrastructure convergence and supply security.

Other Notable Themes:

  • **Trade Policy:** US tariffs and their impact on global trade flows remain a key discussion point, with the Supreme Court ruling and new tariffs creating uncertainty.
  • **Inflation & Rates:** The Fed is signaling restraint on rate cuts, requiring sustained improvement in inflation. Pricing pressures are re-emerging in some sectors (e.g., durable goods), testing the Fed's resolve.
  • **Labor Market:** A slowdown in unauthorized immigration is linked to weaker job growth in construction and manufacturing, potentially impacting housing supply.
  • **Consumer Spending:** While the consumer is active, general merchandise is slowing, and lower-income shoppers are pulling back. GLP-1 drugs are also impacting food consumption patterns.
  • **Private Credit:** Concerns are rising over risks in the private credit market, with some funds restricting withdrawals.
