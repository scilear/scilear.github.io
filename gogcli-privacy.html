gogcli — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-01-29

Summary

gogcli is an open-source command-line tool that runs entirely on your machine. When you use gogcli to access Google services, the CLI communicates directly with Google APIs on your behalf. gogcli does not transmit your Google data to third-party servers.

Data we access (examples)

  • Google Account email and profile (openid, userinfo.email)
  • Gmail messages and metadata (read/send/modify)
  • Gmail settings (basic settings, delegation/sharing)
  • Google Calendar events
  • Contacts
  • Google Drive files and Google Docs/Sheets content when requested

Why we access data

We access only the data necessary to perform actions you explicitly request via the CLI (for example: send or label an email, create a calendar event, list or modify Drive files, read/write Docs/Sheets). The CLI never acts without an explicit user command.

How we store data

OAuth refresh/access tokens: stored locally on your machine by gogcli (default path: ~/.config/gogcli/keyring). Depending on configuration, tokens may be encrypted by your OS keychain or protected with a local passphrase. Tokens remain until you revoke access or remove them locally.

Sharing and third parties

gogcli does not share your Google account data with third parties. All API requests originate from your machine using your own Google credentials.

Security

Use your operating system keyring when available (recommended) to encrypt tokens. If you choose a file-based keyring or passphrase, keep the passphrase secret and protect your user account and filesystem permissions.

Revocation / deletion

  • Revoke from Google: Google Account → Security → Third‑party apps with account access → remove "gogcli".
  • Remove locally: gog auth remove youremail@example.com or delete ~/.config/gogcli/keyring tokens.
